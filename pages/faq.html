Frequently Asked Questions

About Brazilian Jiu Jitsu (BJJ)

What makes Brazilian Jiu Jitsu (BJJ) special when compared to many other martial arts?

BJJ is one of the very few martial arts that has been proven to be effective in real-life situations. It teaches how to overcome bigger and stronger opponents using techniques that actually work!

I'm a woman, is BJJ a good fit for me?

Absolutely! BJJ is the single most effective martial art for women to learn. It will teach you practical self-defence techniques that will empower you to be more confident and resilient.

Is there kicking and/or punching in BJJ?

No. BJJ is a grappling-based martial art that teaches how to deescalate an altercation by using techniques that allow for control and escapes, without the need to hurt your opponent.

Do you teach takedowns at your school?

Yes. Everyone in both the Kids/Teens as well as the Adult Programs gets to learn how to take the fight to the ground as well as how to prevent being taken down, using both wrestling and Judo techniques.

What does Gi & No-Gi mean?

Gi is the traditional uniform, which consists of a jacket, pants and a ranked belt.

No-Gi is when you wear regular exercise clothes such as a rashguard/t-shirt and shorts, but without a ranked belt.

When it comes to practicing BJJ, the fundamental movements generally the same for both. However, the amount of techniques and the way they are performed can have different variations.

What are the ranks in BJJ?

The kids/teens ranks (ages 4-15), and their primary colours are: White -> Grey -> Yellow -> Orange -> Green

The adults ranks are (ages 16+) and their primary colours are: White -> Blue -> Purple -> Brown -> Black.

Additionally, each belt colour generally requires 4 stripes to progress to the next one.

The time you spend at each rank will depend on your level of commitment and proficiency of the martial art.

How long are your classes?

Classes are 1 hour for the following programs: Kids, Teens, & Beginners.

Only the All-Levels program is 1.5 hours long.

SAFETY

Do I have to be physically fit to participate?

No. All of our programs are designed to help you ease into every exercise/technique. We will never force you to do anything that you are not comfortable with. Our goal is to only make you stronger and healthier, while making sure that you are having a good time doing so.

I am # of years old, is it too late to start?

Absolutely NOT! BJJ is commonly practiced by people all over the world and by all age groups! It is not unusual to see students who are as young as 5 years old as well as those who are 60+.

We understand that starting something new can be intimidating at first, but we promise to make you feel safe.

I'm worried about injuries, is Jiu Jitsu a good fit for me?

When doing any physical activity, there is always a chance that you might get a few bumps and bruises. At our gym, we always make sure that your safety is our #1 priority, so that serious injuries do not happen.

We take extra care in keeping everyone safe by providing clear instructions and discouraging any aggressive/uncontrolled training behaviour. You can rest assured that we will do everything we can for you to feel safe and taken care of.

PROMOTIONS & GRADINGS

How do you decide when someone is ready for a promotion?

Our general approach is to promote adults and kids based on their knowledge and performance - never on just attendance. With that said, we do factor in any existing learning, physical or other limitations.

I already have a rank in BJJ, will you recognize it?

Absolutely! You worked hard and put in the time. Whatever rank you have been given at another gym, we will honour it.

Do I have to compete at tournaments?

No. As much as we value competition, we understand that most of our students are doing BJJ as a fun activity.

For those students that are interested in testing their skills through competition, we will support them in their training and make sure that they are prepared to do their best.
